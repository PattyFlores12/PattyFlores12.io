PATTY'S FLOWERS

ABOUT ME

What started the tradition

Hello everyone, my name is Patrick Milligan. Growing up I had an affinity for superheroes and fell in love with helping others. I always wanted to be the person that people can rely on and know that they can go to. After high school, I made a promise to myself that I was going to hold these values even closer to my heart and change the world with these values. When I finally got to UTA I got as involved as possible to immerse myself into the student body. Following my first semester, I saw that the university didn't have anything planned for the students for Valentine's Day, so I jumped into action! I quickly got onto all of my social media and did my best to raise some money for flowers. I was only able to raise about $50, so I used my own money to help purchase about 400 flowers. From 8am to 3pm, I handed out all the flowers on Valentine's Day.

Patrick Milligan


This year I'm getting a head start; my goal is to raise about $500 (if not more) for flowers for this upcoming Valentine's Day (Hopefully by Feb. 10th). Whatever is leftover from the money for the flowers, I am going to donate to the fraternity on my campus, Beta Theta Pi, in honor of their international philanthropy (they dress up as superheroes for kids in hospitals!). I can't explain how much this would mean to me; people ask me, "Patty, why do you do things like this?" All I can say is this: you never know who is having a bad day, if you have the power to make someone's day you should act on it. Having super powers or a super suit doesn't make you a superhero, doing something "because its the right thing to do, is indeed without a doubt [what makes] a real superhero." (-Stan Lee)
